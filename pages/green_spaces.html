Introduction & Objectives

A Conceptual Analysis of Green Spaces in Kirchheim unter Teck This project explores the distribution, accessibility, and significance of green free spaces in the suburban municipality of Kirchheim unter Teck. It examines how these spaces contribute to the happiness and well-being of residents, focusing on the interplay between private, shared, and public free spaces. The study combines qualitative and quantitative methods to assess the spatial and atmospheric qualities of these spaces, aiming to provide insights into their role as places of mental and physical contentment. By analyzing urban patterns across selected neighborhoods, the project offers a deeper understanding of suburban green infrastructure and its implications for quality of life.

Objectives

  • Map the distribution of green free spaces in Kirchheim unter Teck.
  • Analyze the accessibility of these spaces by various means of transport.
  • Summarize the typical free space structure in suburban areas based on findings

Results & Discussion

The findings illustrate the typology of green spaces in Kirchheim under Teck. Each image highlights specific aspects such as the distribution of forests, orchards, parks, and recreation areas, providing insights into their spatial structure and accessibility.

The analysis suggests that forests dominate the green spaces, contributing significantly to the local ecosystem. Orchards and parks are evenly distributed across neighborhoods, enhancing recreational opportunities. Future planning should focus on improving the accessibility of nature reserves and cemeteries.
