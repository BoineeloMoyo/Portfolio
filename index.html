About Me

Geospatial Developer

My name is Boineelo, which means Dedication. I hold an M.Sc in Geoinformatics and a double major BA in Environmental Sciences and Archaeology – a unique mix, I know! I’m a dedicated geospatial developer and a certified Environmental Practitioner with the Botswana Environmental Assessment Practitioners Board (EAPB) as well as a professional archaeologist registered with the Botswana National Museum.

Currently, I work as a Data Scientist at the Thünen Institute in Germany, where I’m immersed in transforming raw alphanumeric and geospatial data into harmonized, structured datasets for scientific research. My work involves handling complex administrative and statistical data to support scientific research at the Thunen Institute. Previously, I was a Junior GIS and Earth Observation Specialist, focusing on machine learning-based land cover classification using high-resolution satellite imagery and producing high-quality geospatial products for thematic analysis.

I’m passionate about applying cutting-edge geospatial techniques to address global challenges like climate change and sustainable resource management. My research interests include Land Use and Land Cover Change, Climate Change Modeling, Energy Efficiency, Environmental and Biodiversity Management, Drought Assessment, Risk and Impact Analysis, and Mitigation.
